Indian Law Basics

Understanding the Indian legal system made simple

üìä Structure of Courts in India

Supreme Court of India

Highest judicial authority. Located in New Delhi. Hears appeals and constitutional matters.

‚Üì

High Courts

28 High Courts across states and union territories. Appellate jurisdiction over district courts.

‚Üì

District Courts

Trial courts at district level. Handle civil and criminal cases at the first instance.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Branches of Law

Civil Law

Deals with disputes between individuals and organizations (property, contracts, torts)

Criminal Law

Relates to crimes and punishment as per Indian Penal Code (IPC)

Constitutional Law

Interpretation and implementation of the Constitution of India

Family Law

Marriage, divorce, child custody, adoption, and inheritance

Property Law

Ownership, transfer, and disputes related to real estate

Corporate Law

Business regulations, company formations, mergers, and compliance

Tax Law

Income tax, GST, and other taxation matters

Labour Law

Employment rights, industrial disputes, and workplace regulations

üõ°Ô∏è Fundamental Rights & Duties

Right to Equality (Articles 14-18)

Equality before law, prohibition of discrimination, abolition of untouchability

Right to Freedom (Articles 19-22)

Freedom of speech, assembly, movement, residence, and profession

Right against Exploitation (Articles 23-24)

Prohibition of trafficking, forced labour, and child labour

Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25-28)

Freedom to practice, profess, and propagate religion

Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29-30)

Protection of language, script, and culture of minorities

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

Right to move Supreme Court for enforcement of fundamental rights

üìú Important Acts

Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860

Main criminal code defining crimes and punishments in India

Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973

Procedural law for administration of criminal law in India

Indian Evidence Act, 1872

Rules regarding admissibility of evidence in court proceedings

Information Technology Act, 2000

Law dealing with cybercrime and electronic commerce

Consumer Protection Act, 2019

Protection of consumer rights and interests

Right to Information Act, 2005

Citizens' right to access information from public authorities
